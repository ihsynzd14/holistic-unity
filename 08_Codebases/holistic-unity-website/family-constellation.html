Understanding Family Constellation

What is Family Constellation?

Family Constellation is a systemic approach developed by German therapist Bert Hellinger in the 1990s, drawing on influences from family systems theory, phenomenological philosophy, and ancestral work. The core insight of Family Constellation is that we are not isolated individuals — we are embedded in a complex web of family and ancestral relationships that profoundly shape our lives.

When something goes unresolved in a family system — unprocessed grief, the exclusion of a member, unacknowledged events, betrayals, or losses — the effects can ripple through generations, manifesting in the lives of descendants as patterns of anxiety, relational difficulties, health challenges, or a sense of not belonging. In a constellation session, the facilitator helps you explore your family system and uncover these hidden dynamics. Through this process of recognition and systemic resolution, a movement toward balance — often described as a "new order" — can naturally emerge.

Online individual constellation sessions are a powerful alternative to group formats, offering the same depth of transformative work in a one-to-one setting. Many clients report profound shifts in their sense of belonging, relationships, and life direction after engaging with Family Constellation work.

The Process

How a Family Constellation Session Works

01

Intake

Share what you'd like to explore (a relationship, pattern, or question) and relevant family history.

02

Setting the Field

Facilitator guides you into a receptive state and begins to explore the system and what is present.

03

Constellation Movement

Using floor anchors, figures, or intuitive sensing, the hidden dynamics and entanglements become visible.

04

Resolution

Facilitator guides movements toward a new, healthier order within the system and you find inner spaciousness.
